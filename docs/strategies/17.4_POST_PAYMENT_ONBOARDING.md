# MS17 Phase 4 Strategy - Post-Payment Onboarding

Status: Pending.
Parent milestone: [`docs/milestones/17_PRODUCT_READINESS.md`](../milestones/17_PRODUCT_READINESS.md)
Depends on: Phase 1 complete (route and variable names will have changed).
Canonical inputs: [`docs/specs/NOTIFICATIONS.md`](../specs/NOTIFICATIONS.md), [`docs/PRODUCT_SPEC.md`](../PRODUCT_SPEC.md)

Owner tags: `[Agent]` = critical-path code and doc work to drive directly. `[Subagent]` = safe delegated sidecar work that can run once prerequisites in this phase are done. `[User]` = work you can drive directly without specialized coaching.

The existing getting-started flow (`GettingStartedFlow`, `GettingStartedController`) has three steps: `wallet → invoice → deliver`. It completes when the issuer has sent their first invoice. This phase adds a fourth step, `receipt`, that activates once a paid invoice is receipt-eligible and completes when the issuer sends the first reviewed client receipt.

The receipt step is conditional: issuers who have not yet had a paid invoice do not see it. It is included in the snapshot only when `receiptStepActive()` returns true, so Part 1 completions that predate any paid invoice do not surface a dangling step.

Part 2 must respect the same rules as the rest of the receipt model: it does not auto-send anything, and the step is not shown as completable when pending ignore/reattribution review would make a receipt untruthful.

## What already exists

- `Invoice::needsReceiptReview()` — returns true when the invoice is paid, has a client email, and has no receipt delivery queued or sent yet. This is the inverse of `receiptStepComplete()`.
- `Invoice::receiptReviewReasons()` — returns human-readable reasons why a receipt may need extra scrutiny (multiple on-chain payments, ignored/reattributed rows). Used to determine whether the step should be blocked.
- `DashboardSnapshot` already surfaces a "receipts waiting for review" prompt on the dashboard.
- `Invoice::canSendReceipt()` — returns true when the invoice is paid and the client has an email address.

## 4. Partial payment guidance (Option B)

When `receiptStepActive()` is false but the issuer has at least one invoice in `partial` status and Part 2 is not yet complete, surface a guidance message in the getting-started flow — specifically on the step view when the deliver step is complete and no receipt step is active. This gives first-time issuers enough context to not be stranded without adding a new completion gate.

The guidance should explain:
- The client sent less than the full amount.
- Options: wait for the remainder, apply a manual credit to close the balance, or contact the client.
- Once the invoice is marked paid, this step will activate automatically.

This message only appears while the getting-started flow is active (Part 2 incomplete). It does not appear after onboarding is done.

1. [ ] [Agent] Add `hasPartialInvoiceWithNoReceipt(User $user): bool` to `GettingStartedFlow` — returns true when the issuer has at least one invoice in `partial` status and `receiptStepActive()` is false. Used as the condition for showing the guidance message.
2. [ ] [Agent] Pass `partialPaymentPending` (bool) to the getting-started step view when the deliver step is complete and the receipt step is inactive.
3. [ ] [Agent] Render the partial payment guidance message in the step view when `partialPaymentPending` is true — inline copy, no new step, no new completion gate.
