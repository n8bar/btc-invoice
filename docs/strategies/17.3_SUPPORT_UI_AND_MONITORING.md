## What already exists

The support UI is substantially built. A support agent is identified by the `SUPPORT_AGENT_EMAILS` allowlist (`config/support.php`), routed to `/support` after login via `AuthenticatedSessionController`, and gated throughout by `EnsureSupportAgent` middleware.

Existing surfaces:
- **Support dashboard** (`/support`) — lists issuers with active grants: name, email, expiry, client/invoice counts, links to their invoices and clients.
- **Issuer invoice index** (`/support/issuers/{issuer}/invoices`) — paginated invoice list for a granted issuer.
- **Issuer invoice detail** (`/support/issuers/{issuer}/invoices/{invoice}`) — full invoice detail with payment history including reattribution and adjustment states.
- **Issuer client index** (`/support/issuers/{issuer}/clients`) — client list for a granted issuer.
- **Issuer client detail** (`/support/issuers/{issuer}/clients/{client}`) — client detail with recent invoices.

All surfaces are read-only. Policies are not modified; support access only flows through dedicated `/support` routes.

## Role framing for RC

For RC, the support agent role doubles as operational maintainer. There is one allowlist (`SUPPORT_AGENT_EMAILS`), one dashboard, and one person (or small team) covering both customer support and system health. This is an explicit RC decision, not an oversight — see backlog item 20 for the post-MVP role separation path.

## What Phase 3 builds

A service health monitoring panel rendered at the top of the support dashboard (`support.dashboard`), above the issuer grants table. The panel queries existing data — no new infrastructure, no external tools.

Three indicators:

**1. Queue depth**
Count of `invoice_deliveries` rows with `status = 'queued'` or `status = 'sending'`. A nonzero count that isn't clearing is a signal the queue worker is stalled.

**2. Recent delivery failures**
Count and list of `invoice_deliveries` rows with `status = 'failed'` in the last 24 hours. Show: delivery type (`type`), recipient, and `error_message`. Helps distinguish transient bounces from systematic failures.

**3. Watcher health**
The most recent `detected_at` (falling back to `created_at`) across all `invoice_payments` rows that are not adjustments (`is_adjustment = false`). Surface the timestamp and flag it visually if it is older than a configurable threshold (default: 60 minutes). A stale timestamp means the blockchain watcher has not recorded any activity recently — not necessarily broken, but worth a look if invoices are pending.
